Directors of photography

Roger Deakins

Roger Alexander Deakins is an English cinematographer. Often regarded as one of the greatest and most influential cinematographers in the history of cinema, he is the recipient of numerous accolades, including two Academy Awards for Best Cinematography and five BAFTA Awards for Best Cinematography.

He has collaborated multiple times with directors such as the Coen brothers, Sam Mendes, and Denis Villeneuve. His best-known works include The Shawshank Redemption (1994), Fargo (1996), O Brother, Where Art Thou? (2000), A Beautiful Mind (2001), Skyfall (2012), Sicario (2015), Blade Runner 2049 (2017), and 1917 (2019), the last two of which earned him Academy Awards.

"I like simplicity. I like using natural sources. I like images to look natural - as though somebody sitting in a room by a lamp is being lit by that lamp."

Hoyte van Hoytema

Hoyte van Hoytema is a Dutch-Swedish cinematographer. Renowned for his handheld camera work and for shooting primarily on film, he has received two Academy Award for Best Cinematography nominations, for the Christopher Nolan–directed films Dunkirk (2017) and Oppenheimer (2023), winning the Oscar for the latter.

"To all aspiring filmmakers out there, I would like to say please try shooting with that incredible, new, hip thing called celluloid. It’s much easier than you think. And it makes things look so much better."

About me

My name is Marta, I am a software developer and, as you can see, I am a photography lover.
I studied illustration and a bit of cinema. I have always been attracted to capture or reproduce anything I find beautiful. And I see beauty in life all the time.
Even though I don't have a passion to create my own art (for the moment), I enjoy admiring other people's art and interpretations of life.

Marta López | Barcelona, 2025
